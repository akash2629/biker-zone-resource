Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Replace your helmet if: It's 5-7 years old. Shows visible damage or cracks. Foam inside is degraded. Doesn't fit properly or has a damaged retention system. Doesn't meet current safety standards. Has been in a crash or sustained a significant impact. Shows signs of general wear and tear.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Inspect your bike for any visible damage, check tire pressure, brakes, and ensure all components are secure. If everything looks good, it should be safe to ride; otherwise, address any issues before riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Follow traffic laws. Wear a helmet. Use hand signals. Stay visible with lights/reflectors. Ride in the same direction as traffic. Be aware of your surroundings. Yield to pedestrians. Avoid distractions. Maintain your bike. Signal and check before turning.
